# Sample Digest Entries

Real-looking examples of filled-in reflections. Use these to understand what good entries look like — not too long, not too vague.

## Example 1 — Strong entry (all three fields)

**Expert:** Shep Hyken  
**Pillar:** Consistency  
**Insight:** "Amazing" isn't about occasional over-the-top moments. It's about being consistently and predictably a little better than average — every single time.

**My Take:**
This reframes everything. I've been celebrating the one brilliant customer interaction my team had, but ignoring the three mediocre ones the same week. The WOW framing has been making me measure the wrong thing.

**Apply to my work:**
Our QA process currently spotlights both the best and worst tickets. I want to shift focus to the middle — are our average interactions reliably good? That's the real question.

**Action:**
This week: pull 10 random tickets (not best/worst) and score them. Is "average" good enough? Report back to the team.

## Example 3 — Pushback (disagreement)

**Expert:** Chris Voss  
**Pillar:** Emotion & Tension  
**Insight:** Label the emotion before offering the solution. "It sounds like this has been really frustrating" — one sentence like this deflates 80% of defensiveness.

**My Take:**
Partially. This works in voice/chat. But in email it can feel formulaic if agents just paste "it sounds like you're frustrated" before every response. The principle is right; the execution needs care. Training needs to cover *when* and *how*, not just *what*.

**Apply to my work:**
In our next QA session: review how agents are handling emotional acknowledgement in email vs. phone. Are they doing it at all? Is it landing as genuine?

**Action:**
Pull 5 email threads where CSAT was < 3. Check: was emotion acknowledged? How?

## What makes a good entry

**My Take:**
- Takes a position — agree, disagree, partially agree
- Adds something the expert didn't say, or names an exception
- Connects to something specific from your experience
- NOT: "this is interesting" / "great point" / restating the insight

**Apply to my work:**
- Names a specific team, process, or situation
- Concrete enough that someone else could understand your context
- NOT: "I should think about this more"

**Action:**
- One thing, with a timeframe ("this week", "next 1:1", "by Friday")
- Small enough to actually do
- NOT: "improve our onboarding" (too big) / "read more about this" (not action)
