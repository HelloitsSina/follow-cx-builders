## 2. Expert Insights Extracted

### Customer Support & Experience

**Jeff Toister** — Conversations · Team Training
- Training must be: Simple, Consistent, Actionable
- Preemptive Acknowledgement: acknowledge the problem before the customer complains
- Counter-intuitive: the more you "take it personally" with angry customers, the better
- 5-5-5 method: 5-min lessons, 5x/week, 5 core skills
- Customer interactions are first-hand product data

**Dan Gingiss** — Real Examples · CX Case Studies
- You don't need to be perfect — you need to be remarkable
- Bad AND good service cases are both teaching material
- Social media replies are public brand statements
- Every service failure is a gift from a competitor

**Adrian Swinscoe** — Voice of Customer → Action
- VoC must be a closed loop: Listen → Act → Feed back
- Most companies have data but lack ability to read customer intent
- Listening must be institutionalised
- Radical honesty: real insights are uncomfortable
- Future CX leaders need both data fluency and people instinct

**Shep Hyken** — Service Culture · "Amazing" CX
- Amazing = consistently and predictably slightly above average (not occasional WOW)
- Managing Un-WOW = as important as creating WOW
- Friction is the #1 loyalty killer
- Agent core skills: empathy, resilience, real listening
- Customers benchmark you against the best experience they've ever had — from anyone
- 2026 data: 96% say product quality > service; 65% say convenience > friendliness
- AI transparency is non-negotiable

### B2B Support / Systems / Scaling

**Daphne Costa Lopes** — SaaS Scale · Processes
- Structure before scale — scaling amplifies everything, good and bad
- CS and Support need clearly defined boundaries
- Knowledge base is infrastructure for scale
- Data-driven decisions: every key call needs data behind it

**Justin Chappell** — Playbooks · Support Orgs
- A Playbook is a decision framework, not a rule book
- Great support org design starts from the customer journey, not current headcount
- Ops value: no one reinvents the wheel
- Change management is a core CX leadership skill
- Goal: 80% of common issues resolved without Manager input

**Carly Agar** — Structured Support · Less Tickets
- The best support is support that never needs to happen
- Deflect > Resolve: ticket reduction = respect for customer time
- Better onboarding cuts support volume 30–50% in first 90 days
- Proactive support: reach out before the problem hits
- Self-serve success = customer finds answer in 3 steps or fewer

**Lisa Stoner** — Operational Efficiency at Scale
- At scale, consistency comes from systems, not individuals
- QA is coaching, not auditing
- Efficiency metrics and quality metrics must coexist
- At scale, Manager must shift from doer to system designer
- Data visibility: problems should be visible before they become crises

### Soft Skills & Communication

**Chris Voss** — FBI Negotiation · Tension Handling
- Tactical empathy: make the other person feel understood (not agreed with)
- Labeling: "It sounds like this has been really frustrating" deflates 80% of defensiveness
- "No" is the beginning of real dialogue, not the end
- Mirroring (repeat last 2–3 words) → more information, more trust
- "That's right" = trust fully established

**Celeste Headlee** — Listening · Presence
- Real listening is not waiting for your turn to speak
- Enter every conversation assuming you can learn something you don't know
- Genuine curiosity changes the texture of a conversation
- Listening quality is a measurable dimension of service

**Vanessa Van Edwards** — Reading People · Rapport
- PQ (People Intelligence) is the new IQ
- 7 universal facial expressions readable across cultures
- Three high-dopamine words: "you", "because", "imagine"
- First impressions form in 7 seconds: voice + eye contact + body language
- Digital cues (email, video) are equally readable

**Nate Brown** — Empathy · Experience Design
- Empathy is a design tool, not just a soft skill
- EX → CX: you cannot create enjoyable CX with miserable employees
- Empathy must be institutionalised — individual goodwill isn't enough
- Start experience design with an emotion map
- Reducing customer effort is more sustainable than creating delight

### Support, Loyalty & Trends

**Adam Topore** — Practical Advice for Agents
- Give the team "why" before "how"
- Empowerment > rules
- Emotional labour must be formally recognised
- Daily positive reinforcement shapes culture more than annual reviews
- Celebrate small wins — it's the foundation of high-performance culture

**Gadi Shamia** — AI & Automation in Support
- AI's value: free humans to focus on what actually needs a human
- Understand the process before automating it — automating a broken process = failing faster
- AI's biggest risk: eroding human connection
- Measure AI success by: did experience improve? (not: did costs go down?)
- Human-in-the-loop is the practical model for the AI transition period
- Prediction: at least one top-10 BPO will file for bankruptcy in 2026 as AI contracts expire

**Bill Quiseng** — Sharp Service Insights
- Service is what you do. Value is what the customer feels.
- Customers don't buy your service — they buy a better version of themselves
- Customer loyalty is earned, not bought
- Simplicity is the most underrated service quality
- Thanking customers = making them feel their time and trust are valued

**Anika Zubair** — Career Growth · Systems Thinking
- CX leaders need T-shaped skills: deep customer insight + broad business understanding
- Personal brand = the asset no company can take from you
- Systems thinking: root cause of recurring problems is almost always upstream
- Managing up is a core CX Manager skill
- Career accelerators: continuous learning + public sharing + community building

## 5. Tool Evolution

### v1 — CX Follow Builder
Full dashboard, 16 expert cards, LinkedIn content generator.  
**Problem:** Too focused on output/sharing, not learning.

### v2 — Digest-first
One card at a time. Sequential. Write your take, save, move to next.  
Inspired by Zara Zhang's follow-builders: signal filtering, not aggregation.

### v3 — Structured Reflection (Chinese)
Added 3 structured input fields per card:
- **My Take** — agree / disagree / what would you add?
- **Apply to my work** — what does this mean for my team right now?
- **Action** — one specific thing, this week

### v4 — English + Source Links + Notion Workflow (Final)
- All 16 insights in English, practitioner tone
- Source link on every card
- `→ notion` tab: 4-step guide to connect via Notion API
- Once credentials set: "save + sync →" auto-creates Notion row

**Notion data model (9 fields):**
```
Name        TITLE
Pillar      SELECT
Insight     TEXT
Source      URL
My Take     TEXT
Apply       TEXT
Action      TEXT
Date        DATE
Status      SELECT  (new / applied / tested)
```

## 7. Weekly Digest Format

Max 10 reads per week. Each item:

```
### N · Expert Name — Article title
**Who:** one sentence on who this person is
**What:** what they said, jargon in plain English
**Why it matters for you:** framed for a CX Team Lead specifically
**One question to sit with:** a reflection prompt
↗ [Read: title](url)
```

Ends with:
- "This week's one thing" — single most important read
- One reflection prompt across all items

**To run:** Message Claude "run the weekly CX digest" — searches past 7 days, picks ≤10, pushes to CX Notion area.

## 9. Reference: Insight Capture Structure

From the Notion document shared during conversation:

**Layer 1 — Basic info**
Title · Source · Expert · Category · Link · Date

**Layer 2 — Thinking conversion (the differentiator)**
- Problem: what does this content solve?
- Insight: one sentence
- Why it matters: in your own words
- **My Take: agree / disagree / what would you add?**

**Layer 3 — Application**
- Apply to my work: how to use this in your current role?
- Action Idea: concrete next steps
- Impact: which metric? (CES / ticket volume / SLA)

**20-minute input rhythm:**
1. Read 5 items, pick 2 with strongest resonance
2. Write 2 insights — one sentence each
3. Write one action for the week
