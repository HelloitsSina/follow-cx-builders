# Building Your CX Framework

The goal of `follow-cx-builders` isn't to finish the digest. It's to build a point of view.

This guide explains how your reflections compound into a personal CX operating principle.

---

## The 5 Pillars

Every insight in the digest maps to one of five strategic dimensions. Together they cover the full scope of what a CX Team Lead needs to think about.

### Pillar 1 — Consistency
*Hyken · Toister · Stoner*

**The core question:** What does "good" look like reliably, not occasionally?

Key tensions to resolve:
- Amazing = consistent baseline vs. occasional WOW moment (Hyken)
- Eliminating Un-WOW vs. creating delight (Hyken)
- Training for consistency vs. empowering judgment (Toister vs. Chappell)

**Your framework question:**
> In my team, what is the single most inconsistent touchpoint? What would it take to make it reliably good?

---

### Pillar 2 — Emotion & Tension
*Voss · Headlee · Van Edwards · Brown*

**The core question:** How does your team handle the hard human moments?

Key tensions to resolve:
- Label emotion first vs. move to resolution efficiently
- Professional distance vs. personal investment (Toister's counter-intuition)
- EX driving CX — are you investing in your team's emotional state?

**Your framework question:**
> What does my team's current SOP look like for an escalation or an angry customer? Where does emotional intelligence enter the process — or does it?

---

### Pillar 3 — Scale & Systems
*Lopes · Chappell · Agar*

**The core question:** What breaks first when your team or volume grows?

Key tensions to resolve:
- Deflect vs. resolve (which deserves more resource?)
- Playbook as rule book vs. decision framework (Chappell)
- Structure before scale — are you building on solid ground?

**Your framework question:**
> If my team doubled tomorrow, which process fails first? Does that problem already exist at smaller scale?

---

### Pillar 4 — Voice → Action
*Swinscoe · Gingiss · Quiseng*

**The core question:** How does customer feedback become real change?

Key tensions to resolve:
- VoC as data collection vs. closed-loop system (Swinscoe)
- Service (what you do) vs. value (what they feel) (Quiseng)
- Remarkable vs. perfect (Gingiss)

**Your framework question:**
> When did I last turn a piece of customer feedback into a concrete change? How long did it take — and who drove it?

---

### Pillar 5 — People & Culture
*Topore · Zubair · Shamia*

**The core question:** What are you doing for the people doing the work?

Key tensions to resolve:
- Why before How (Topore) — does your team understand the reason behind the rules?
- AI freeing humans vs. AI eroding connection (Shamia vs. Headlee)
- Root cause upstream vs. fixing at the support layer (Zubair)

**Your framework question:**
> What friction points in my team's daily work are quietly draining their enthusiasm? Do I actually know what they are?

## How to synthesise

After you've filled in reflections across at least 3–5 digests, you have enough raw material.

### Option A — Use the tool
Click "synthesize my thinking" in the digest tool. It reads your entries and generates:
1. Your emerging CX philosophy (2–3 sentences)
2. Your clearest strength (what theme appears most)
3. One unresolved tension (where your takes contradict each other)
4. One blind spot (what you've barely touched)

### Option B — Do it yourself (10 minutes)

Read back through your My Take entries. Then answer:

```
MY CX OPERATING PRINCIPLE
(2-3 sentences — what do I actually believe about great CX?)


MY CLEAREST CONVICTION
(the thing I believe most strongly, that I'd argue for)


THE TENSION I HAVEN'T RESOLVED
(two things I believe that pull in opposite directions)


MY BLIND SPOT
(the pillar I've barely engaged with — and why)


MY NORTH STAR FOR HARD DECISIONS
(when I'm not sure what to do, I ask myself:)
```

## Keeping it alive

A framework that doesn't evolve is just theory.

After every weekly digest, ask:
- Does anything I read this week shift my operating principle?
- Did I actually do the action I logged last week?
- Is my Status updating in Notion (new → applied → tested)?

Every quarter, revisit your CX Operating Principle. Edit it. The goal isn't to finalise it — it's to notice it changing as you gain experience.

---

## Signs your framework is working

- You refer to it when making a decision (not just when writing about it)
- You disagree with experts more specifically — not just "I think differently" but "Hyken says X but in my context Y because Z"
- Your team can articulate elements of your philosophy without you explaining it
- You're bored of the beginner content and looking for harder questions
